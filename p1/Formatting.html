Space exploration

Space exploration is the use of astronomy and space technology to explore outer space.While the
exploration of space is currently carried out mainly by astronomers with telescopes, its physical exploration is
conducted both by uncrewed robotic space probes and human spaceflight. Space exploration, like its classical
form astronomy, is one of the main sources for space science.

While the observation of objects in space, known as astronomy, predates reliable recorded history, it was the
development of large and relatively efficient rockets during the mid-twentieth century that allowed physical
space exploration to become a reality.The world's first large-scale experimental rocket program was Opel
RAK under the leadership of Fritz Opel and Max Valier durning the late 1920s leading to the first manned
rocket cars and rocket planes, which paved the way for the Nazi era V2 program and US and Soviet activities
from 1950 onwards.
